## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Verdict:** Clean. No AI-generated slop detected.

- **LLM Assessment**: The design avoids modern SaaS clichés (no rounded cards, no glassmorphism blurs, no generic gradients). The use of monospaced body copy and dashed borders gives the page a distinct, authoritative, research-journal personality.
- **Deterministic Scan**: The automated detector scanned `app/page.tsx` and returned 0 violations.
- **Visual Overlays**: No visual overlays were injected.

## Overall Impression

The page achieves a beautiful, quiet, scholarly look that matches the "Archive Ink" brand personality. Sourcing and credibility are treated with high respect. The main opportunity lies in sharpening text contrast for small monospace fonts and adding power-user shortcuts for research navigation.

## What's Working

1. **Tactile Print Aesthetic**: The dashed boundaries (`border-dashed`), thin rules, and monospace type successfully emulate physical documents.
2. **Clear Content-Led Flow**: The page leads with a direct value proposition, flows into recent articles, then provides the methodology explanation, keeping the focus entirely on content.

## Priority Issues

### [P2] Small Monospace Readability
- **Why it matters**: Monospace body descriptions (like in cards and the "Why NaLI" section) are small (`text-[0.78rem]`, `text-[0.82rem]`) and use `text-gray` (lower contrast). This can cause eye strain on longer sentences.
- **Fix**: Boost body text size slightly and change the color to `text-ink-charcoal` for high contrast.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable typeset`

### [P2] Missing Keyboard Search Accelerator
- **Why it matters**: Researchers and power users ("Alex") expect keyboard-based search triggers (e.g. `⌘K` or `/`) to navigate articles quickly, but there is no keyboard indicator next to the search icon.
- **Fix**: Add a small monospace shortcut indicator (e.g. `[K]`) next to the search icon.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable delight`

### [P3] Minor Hover Inconsistencies
- **Why it matters**: Sibling CTAs have slightly different hover treatments (some swap background/text, others apply `bg-ink-wash`).
- **Fix**: Standardize on a unified interactive transition scale.
- **Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout`

## Persona Red Flags

- **Alex (Power User)**: Forced to click the search trigger icon directly. No keyboard shortcut indicator displayed in the header. High risk of friction when trying to quickly find sources.
- **Jordan (First-Timer)**: Primary CTA is "Baca artikel", which drops Jordan into the list. If Jordan skips the "Kenapa NaLI" section, they might miss the core value prop of "Confidence Labels".
- **Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: Small buttons ("Ruang Kendali", "Buka arsip") are placed closely together at the bottom of the page, making one-handed thumb taps error-prone on smaller viewports.

## Minor Observations

- The fallback background for `public/videos/hero.mp4` is neat, but the page would feel more dynamic with a subtle background shader or video element loaded.
- The footer has good spacing, but could feature a cleaner copyright/licensing layout.
