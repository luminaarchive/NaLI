# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Indonesian readers, researchers, and citizens interested in nature, history, and investigative stories based on open archives and datasets. They expect high credibility, rigorous sourcing, and a reading environment that feels scholarly, immersive, and clear.

## Product Purpose
NaLI by NatIve is an open research journal covering nature, history, and investigation in Indonesia. It serves as a repository for deep, source-backed articles, field notes, and archives, combining open science, archival research, and investigative journalism.

## Brand Personality
Scholarly & Inquisitive. The tone is authoritative, objective, and deeply respectful of Indonesian history and natural heritage. Visually, this is represented by deep charcoal and off-white tones with sharp teal accents, clean editorial typography, and prominent reliability/confidence indicators.

## Anti-references
Generic SaaS templates: avoid soft drop-shadow ghost cards, rounded card/input boundaries, tiny uppercase eyebrows, and generic purple/blue gradients. Cliché blog designs with busy sidebars, card-grid layouts, and low-contrast grey text.

## Design Principles
1. Source First: Design around reliability, citations, and trust markers. Sourcing is the hero of the content.
2. Editorial Restraint: Prioritize high-contrast readability, generous whitespace, and balanced typography. Let the research speak for itself.
3. Immersive Focus: Eliminate unnecessary decorative elements (like generic blurs, doodles, or gradients) in favor of clear, content-focused layouts.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Ensure WCAG AA contrast compliance for all text against backgrounds (especially deep charcoal and light backgrounds). Support reduced motion preferences across all page transitions and micro-interactions.
