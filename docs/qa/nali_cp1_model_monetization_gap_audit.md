# Audit: NaLI CP1 Model Monetization Gap (V7 Baseline)

## Scope

This audit records why the V7 rich-evidence output was insufficient for CP1 monetization differentiation before the V8 implementation. It reviews local founder/admin QA artifacts in `~/Downloads/NaLI-QA/`; it does not activate public export, checkout, source verification, or upload.

## V7 Baseline Finding

The three V7 artifacts were substantially overlapping products rather than distinct tiers:

| V7 output | PDF pages | Material differentiation problem                                                                |
| --------- | --------: | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Peregrine |         6 | Rendered a journal-shaped output with both major figure treatments and a rich section skeleton. |
| Obsidian  |         8 | Added seriousness in prose, but did not establish a decisive audit capability moat.             |
| Zephyr    |         8 | Shared the same broad length class and visual/document skeleton as Obsidian.                    |

Peregrine was too close to a full journal output for a starter model. It exposed too much of the value expected from paid tiers: long-form presentation, rich fixture content, and a publication-like artifact. Obsidian and Zephyr were both eight-page outputs, so the premium tier did not visibly earn its highest positioning.

## Monetization Gaps

1. **Peregrine ceiling missing.** A starter user could receive an output visually close enough to a premium draft that an upgrade was difficult to justify.
2. **Obsidian moat weak.** Evidence sufficiency, cannot-conclude analysis, data risks, and methodological vulnerability were not presented as a clearly owned audit product.
3. **Zephyr pricing power weak.** It lacked exclusive editorial structure, premium presentation, and visible long-form refinement over Obsidian.
4. **UI upgrade reason unclear.** Previous selector language did not set an honest expectation for what each tier provides or intentionally omits.
5. **Cost/value hierarchy unsupported.** A higher-cost profile is not defensible when pages, tables, figures, and editorial experience appear nearly interchangeable.

## Required Boundary Decision

The V7 conclusion is: **current differentiation is insufficient for monetization**.

| Tier      | Mandatory product boundary                                                                                                                   |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Peregrine | Hard-capped starter brief or short practicum note; compact output, one primary figure, no full evidence audit or premium editorial sections. |
| Obsidian  | Evidence/audit product with sufficiency assessment, cannot-conclude boundaries, risk register, and methodological vulnerability.             |
| Zephyr    | Premium journal draft with integrated editorial flow, exclusive revision/readiness controls, refined captioning, and the richest layout.     |

The capability boundaries must be enforced in the model registry, article construction, HTML/PDF and DOCX renderers, UI copy, estimated-credit language, and automated tests. Any upgrade nudge must state real capability differences and must not claim payment is active.

## Integrity Limits

- All V7 and V8 fixtures are local QA materials only and remain unverified.
- No artifact may claim verified citations, DOI, ISSN, species identity, GPS, images, or evidence.
- Public/user PDF and DOCX export remains locked and inactive.
- Midtrans, paid launch, upload, and source verification remain inactive.
