## Visual QA

All 25 V8 PDF pages were rasterized to temporary QA previews and visually inspected. Result: **PASS**.

- Peregrine is visibly compact, with a starter badge, restrained cover, one table, one figure, and its non-blocking upgrade notice.
- Obsidian uses a more serious audit presentation with evidence inventory, measurement tables, figure plates, sufficiency assessment, cannot-conclude panel, and data risks.
- Zephyr has the most polished cover and opener, refined figure treatment, an exclusive editorial traceability page, a premium reviewer-readiness table, revision notes, annexure, and editorial integrity finish.
- No clipping, page overlap, missing badges, or broken figure/table layout was observed in rendered PDFs.
- Automated local `/create-report` inspection confirmed the compact workspace shows the mode, template, composer, tier buttons, and tier estimate without overlap; each tier disclosure exposes its distinct capability and limitation copy on demand.
- A privacy regression found during local QA was corrected: the NaLI Energy balance lookup sends its guest identifier in a POST body rather than a URL query parameter, preventing normal request logs from exposing that value.

DOCX binaries were generated and structurally exercised by automated tests. Visual DOCX rendering could not be completed in this environment because the required LibreOffice `soffice` executable is not installed; this is recorded as a local QA tooling limitation, not a public feature activation.

It exclusively supplies the long-form editorial experience: executive editorial summary, editorial abstract, integrated literature framing and discussion, premium conclusion, publication-style revision notes, reviewer-readiness controls, refined captions, editorial traceability figure, premium table, and longest polished PDF treatment.

Obsidian no longer competes as a slightly longer starter answer. It exclusively delivers the practical evidence-audit layer over Peregrine: full measurement presentation, sufficiency assessment, cannot-conclude analysis, data risk register, methodological vulnerability, source gaps, and citation-boundary audit.
