# NaLI CP1 Journal V5 Failure Audit

Date: 2026-05-25
Scope: founder/admin local QA output only; public PDF/DOCX export remains locked.
Decision: **FAIL for visual resemblance and founder-approved publication quality.**

## Benchmark And Inspection Evidence

The supplied JWC article was used only as a structural and visual benchmark. Its E-Palli/JWC identity, logo, ISSN, DOI, publisher details, and artwork are not assets for NaLI reuse.

Visual inspection compared the reference cover and article first page with the rendered v5 PDF pages. Text extraction was used only to quantify visible operational language:

The reference uses a bright, full-page visual cover with a strong issue block and composed lower illustration, followed by a dense article first page that begins body text below the abstract. V5 uses a dark sparse cover, a large blank lower region on the article opener, and repeated QA/status signals in the design itself.

## Failure Summary

V5 is **PARTIAL for document structure**: it includes a cover, opener, columns, tables, figure location, annexure, and references handling. V5 is nevertheless **FAIL for visual/professional similarity** and is not acceptable as founder-approved journal output.

The defects requiring V6 correction are:

- Replace the text-led cover with an original full-page nature composition and credible volume/issue block.
- Remove operational absence statements and founder/admin QA wording from prominent article furniture.
- Keep truth constraints in a compact, unobtrusive note only.
- Convert the figure area from a conspicuous empty-status card into a journal-style reserved plate with restrained missing-photo labeling.
- Make article categories visibly distinct through category labels, editorial notes, and substantively expanded prose.
