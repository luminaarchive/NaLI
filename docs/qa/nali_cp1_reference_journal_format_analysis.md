# Reference Journal Layout & Structure Analysis

This document analyzes the layout, structure, and typography of the reference journal article ([E-Palli JWC Article Reference](https://journals.e-palli.com/home/index.php/jwc/article/view/6482/3056)) to establish the structural benchmarks for NaLI CP1's professional journal-style draft generator.

---

## 1. Reference Journal Structural Components

The reference layout is characterized by a multi-layered academic document structure designed for high-density information display:

### A. Cover Page (Journal Issue level)
*   **Journal Title**: Prominently displayed at the top with large serif/sans-serif fonts (e.g., *Journal of Wildlife and Conservation*).
*   **Visual theme**: Large, centered high-resolution photo or graphic showcasing nature/wildlife themes.
*   **Metadata Box**: Volume, Issue, Month, and Year details clearly marked.
*   **Publisher Block**: Publisher identification at the bottom of the cover page.
*   **Page Boundary**: Distinct border style or background layout setting it apart as a booklet/journal cover.

### B. First Article Page
*   **Running Journal Banner**: Top-header row containing the publisher's banner name, DOI, URL, and publication dates.
*   **Main Title Block**: Centered, large bold title with clear spacing.
*   **Author Names & Affiliations**: Sub-header containing authors' names and corresponding superscript numbers linking to their department/institution details and corresponding email.
*   **Article History Box**: Key milestones (Received date, Accepted date, Published date).
*   **Abstract Block**: A shaded, single-column block with margins set narrower than the main text, acting as a compact summary.
*   **Keywords Box**: 3 to 6 descriptive tags placed immediately beneath the abstract.

### C. Body Pages (IMRaD Structure)
*   **Running Header**: Small metadata line containing shortened journal title, year, volume, and authors' names (e.g., *J. Wild. Conserv. 2026*).
*   **Section Headers**: Left-aligned, numbered headings (e.g., **1. INTRODUCTION**, **2. LITERATURE REVIEW**, **3. METHODOLOGY**, **4. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION**, **5. CONCLUSION**).
*   **Two-Column or Dense Single-Column Text**: Dense, formal academic language utilizing consistent line heights, clean letter-spacing, and clear paragraph indents.
*   **Visual Figure Slots**: Outlined boxes with captioned labels (e.g., *Figure 1: Title of the figure...*) centered on the page.
*   **Zebra-Striped Tables**: Formal borders, shaded header rows, and well-aligned cells presenting comparison matrices.

### D. Closing Sections & Appendix
*   **Conflict of Interest & Acknowledgment**: Explicit statements regarding funding and disclosures.
*   **References List**: Alphabetical or numbered bibliographic entries in APA/Harvard styling containing title, authors, journal, volume, page range, and DOI.

## 2. NaLI-Specific Translation & Adaptation

To avoid copyright issues, publisher misrepresentation, and fake academic claims, NaLI adapts these reference structures using a dedicated NaLI-branded theme:

| Reference Component | JWC / E-Palli Implementation | NaLI CP1 Adaptation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Branding & Logos** | JWC & E-Palli logos and layouts | **NaLI Nature & Evidence Journal** (NaLI teal/dark slate theme) |
| **ISBN / ISSN** | Real registered ISSNs | **ISSN: Not applicable — CP1 internal QA draft** |
| **DOI** | Real registered DOIs | **DOI: Not assigned in CP1 (Draft stage)** |
| **Publisher** | E-Palli Publications | **NatIve Field Intelligence Services** |
| **Author Details** | Real researchers & affiliations | **Generated draft from user-provided materials** |
| **Article History** | Received/Accepted/Published timestamps | **Received: N/A** / **Accepted: N/A** / **Published: Draft Recovery Snapshot** |
| **References** | Real scientific citations | **References supplied by the user only**. No generated placeholders or fake DOIs. |
| **Visual Evidence** | Real photos and field data | **Evidence card placeholders** highlighting missing photo uploads and measurement sheets. |
